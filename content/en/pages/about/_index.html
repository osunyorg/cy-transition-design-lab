How can we  inform stakeholders about the current and evolving state of the systems in which they live, and on which they can rely (what is already in place, what works) or which they want to remedy (what needs to be changed)? The research focuses as much on system representations as on design methodologies. We analyze and design representations and visualizations of systems in their current state for heterogeneous audiences and a diversity of purposes: data visualization, information vizualizations, systemic design

How ecological awareness can take hold and spread? The research focuses on identifying the "concerned" (Peneau, 2023), on the ways of capturing and maintaining their attention, and on what supports sustainable behaviors.

We need to dentify the new entrants, human and non-human (Favret-Saada, 1977), (Favret-saada, 2009), (Latour, 2017), and their roles in decision-making (Latour, 2018) (Karana et al., 2020) We investigate the issues of opinion leadership and influence with a focus on actors as well as media (Rogers & Cartano, 1962), Roger 1983, Flynn, Goldsmith, 1996, Valente, Pumpuang, 2007); Akrich, M., M. Callon, and B. Latour. 2002)

We design and organize new format of engagement and feedback: nudge (Thaler & Sunstein, 2009), (Ölander & Thøgersen, 2014) positive reinforcement and gamification theory, engagement management (Andriof et al., 2017)....

Elaborating on participatory design, how can we design for fruitful debates and decision making processes involving a heterogeneous range of "concerned" parties.

o how do we prepare for debates? stakeholder mapping (Reed et al., 2009), controversy mapping (Venturini, 2010), representativeness and governance formats (Berthet & Hickey, 2018)

o how do we design debate modalities? design for debate, agonistic design (Gentes & Mollon, 2015). (Bonaccorsi & julliard, 2012)

o how do we ensure the material and human conditions for a fair debate? (Umney & Lloyd, 2018), (Meadowcroft, 2009),(Gentes & Perez, 2022)

o how do we finalize debates and move on to decisions: rethink negotiation and voting methods (e. g. quadratic voting, (Lalley & Weyl, 2018)

What are the tools and media that enable heterogeneous audiences to project themselves intimately and collectively into possible futures: from frescoes to foresight narratives co-created with AI, to simulation tools?

o Identify best practices (Sio et al., 2015): define their potential for reproducibility and scalability (avoid lists of examples).

o Code scenarios (Bornes et al., 2022), imaginaries and projections of possible futures (Gentes, 2015) (Young et al., 2001, Gentes, 2008, (Heinonen & Hiltunen, 2012), design simulations (weak emergence versus strong emergence (Bedau & Humphreys, 2008)

o Identify alignment spaces to organize flows, organize reproducibility and scaling (Marcocchia 2019, Tura et al. 2018)

o Choose the possible future (benchmark the elements to be emulated).

What are  the media, communication situations and actors likely to define and implement desirable futures? We need to critically question the nature of KPIs and designs evaluation interfaces, accounting instruments, dashboards for activities.

o how to analyze and design evaluation methodologies: criteria, values (Sterman, 2001) (Rodriguez et al., 2019)

o how to dentify the actors and resources needed ( Ostenwalder &Pigneur 2010) to counter the obstacles to the desired future.

o how to identify the stages of organizational transformation based on the dimensions of organization design (Galbraith&Kates, 2010)

o How to draw up an action plan with tools for evaluating the stages and the values and impacts created.

CY Transition Design research relies both on design studies and design science. 

First, we analyze the material culture of the design milieu from an anthropological standpoint. In particular, we focus on the informational literacies and communicational infrastructures that support the mediation between actors in transition. Second, we analyze how artefacts and actors “work together” to achieve innovative goals.

The "Actors / Mediums / Cultures" model (AMC Design model) studies design activities as both tangible and symbolic operations between actors, media and cultures. The AMC Design model highlights 

  • the fundamental role of the "mediums" of conception, debate and decision, 

  • the specificities of the actors’ cultures (aesthetics, social and political values, and modes of legitimization).
